About

Vincent Van Gogh cathartically expresses himself relentlessly through an oil painting in 1890, capturing his post-impressionistic style in Sorrowing Old Man ("At Eternity's Gate"). With the light from the flames in the fireplace coursing over the unembellished walls, a man wearing the darkest color of blue is restlessly seated in a chair in his uninviting room, illustrating the everyday struggles with depression.
